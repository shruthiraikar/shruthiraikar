Labrador

The Labrador Retriever’s strong physique and open, happy temperament make it one of the more tireless breeds.
If you want a breed that makes training easy, that’s very intelligent,balanced and, above all, playful, the Labrador Retriever is for you.

Key features

  • Needs alot of space
  • Needs alot of exercise
  • Friendly with kids
  • life expectancy: 10-14yrs

Beagle

When socialised and exercised well Beagles are good with other pets and children, which combine to make them a popular family breed.
Beagles were initially bred as hunting dogs and they can easily become distracted when out on walks.
This makes Beagles one of the more difficult dog breeds to train.

Key features

  • Needs alot of training
  • Needs alot of exercise
  • Great family dog
  • life expectancy:12-15 yrs

Cocker Spaniel

Naturally cheerful, Cocker Spaniels are active dogs, especially on a trail.
Cocker Spaniels are affectionate and gentle with those they know.
They are adaptable dogs who settle well into the family home. These short,compact, vigorous and well balanced dog are noted for their happy deposition.

Key features

  • Requires alot of grooming
  • Enjoys training
  • Excellent companion for active owners
  • Life expectancy:12-14yrs

German Shepherd

When well trained German Shepherds can be even tempered, self-assured, vigilant,and highly intelligent.
This long list of qualities makes them good companions and protectors, and excellent guarding and herding dogs.
German Shepherds are driven by their motivation to work. They can become a dominant figure in their owners’ lives.

Key features

  • Makes a versatile working dog
  • Needs alot of physical and mental exercise
  • Makes a great family dog
  • Life expectancy:9-13years

Chow Chow

One of the most distinctive features of the Chow Chow is their unusual blue-black tongue. These calm dogs – well suited to guarding – have a number of unique characteristics, one of which is their lion-like appearance and confident gait.
Chow Chows are independent characters, yet very loyal to their owners.
These dogs can be suspicious of strangers.

Key features

  • Needs an owner with some experience
  • Makes a great guard dog
  • Needs alot of training
  • Life expectancy:9-15 years
